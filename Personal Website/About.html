My Source Code

Computer Systems have fascinated me ever since I was young. I still remember the moments I was voluntold to be my home's unofficial I.T. department when our internet would go down or our computer would get a virus. I remember feeling like a hacker as the Earthlink or Digis tech would walk me through running the command line over the phone or pouring over our system's programs until I identified that one file that was infected. That fascination led me to doing some free coding courses online and feeling invigorated.

Two years into college, I decided to finally pursue that passion and switched my major from Technical Theater to Computer Science. The juxtaposition of switching from art to engineering was intimidating because I felt like I was somewhere I didn't belong but I kept a positive attitude and kept telling myself "you CAN do this". This drive and passion has taken me through my education at Weber State University where I plan to graduate in August. They have provided me with an invaluable education in Computer Science with expert instruction in object-oriented programming, operating systems, web development, graphics programming, higher mathematics, Computer Theory, networking and network security, and more. I still carry the creativity I learned through a lifelong passion for theater and acting with me as it has made me a better developer and given me excellent interpersonal communication skills.

This career choice has been the most challenging and humbling journey I've been on but he challenge makes me hunger for more. I am eager to apply my passion and creativity in Industry. There is always more to learn and I feel I have a lot to bring to the table as a developer.

Passions

Acting

I have been an actor almost all my life. My family was heavily into theater and so, naturally, so was I. The passion began in a small community theater in Custer, South Dakota and was heavily influenced by my older brother who stayed the course and is now a professional actor in Washington, D.C. In addition to being a theater kid all through Elementary, Middle, and High School, I spent my summers attending an acting camp at the Utah Shakespeare Festival in Cedar City, Utah where we were trained working professional actors. Apart from being in Annie Get Your Gun at the Ogden Egyptian Theater, my passion for acting was put on hold since changing my major to Computer Science but I hope to get back on the horse when I graduate.

Fitness

Fitness is one of my biggest passions ever since high school when I took a weight lifting class. Since then I have found pleasure in studying fitness and nutrition and trying different kinds of fitness. I'm fascinated by the human body and how different foods and exercise techniques affect the way it functions. It's amazing how our understanding of these two topic is evolving year by year.

Travel

Travelling has always been a huge part of my life. Growing up, my family traveled to many places across the US and abroad. My parents being employees of the Forest Service and lovers of the outdoors led them to instill in us that same love and passion. They loved taking us to National Parks across the country with their favorites being Glacier, Yellowstone, Arches, Bryce Canyon, Fort Jefferson, Zion, and Olympic. My family also had a love of history so we spent many summers traveling up and down the east coast to various Civil War and Revolutionary War hotspots. I have also been to Hawaii, the Bahamas, Canada, France, the U.K., Germany, and the Netherlands. Travel is something that is engrained in my soul and I can't live without.

Learning

In the past couple years, I have discovered an interest in learning. Whether it be DIY skills, history, science, medicine, software or web skills, philosophy, among other topics, I have a thirst for knowledge. Although being a college student makes finding time for other interests difficult.
